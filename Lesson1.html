üìö Basic Accounting Terms

Assets

Resources owned by a business that have economic value (cash, inventory, equipment, property)

Liabilities

Obligations or debts owed by the business to others (loans, accounts payable, mortgages)

Equity

The owner's stake in the business (Assets - Liabilities = Equity)

‚öñÔ∏è Accounting Conventions

Conventions are general agreements that guide accounting practices:

1. Convention of Consistency

Accounting methods should remain consistent from period to period for comparability

2. Convention of Disclosure

All material information must be disclosed in financial statements

3. Convention of Conservatism

When in doubt, choose the option that is least likely to overstate assets or income

4. Convention of Materiality

Only significant items that affect decision-making need detailed reporting

üìã Accounting Standards

Formal guidelines that ensure consistency and transparency in financial reporting

GAAP (Generally Accepted Accounting Principles)

Common set of accounting principles used primarily in the United States. Developed by the Financial Accounting Standards Board (FASB).

IFRS (International Financial Reporting Standards)

Global accounting standards used in over 140 countries. Issued by the International Accounting Standards Board (IASB).

üè¢ Types of Business Ownership

1. Sole Proprietorship

Owner: Single individual
Liability: Unlimited personal liability
Tax: Income taxed as personal income
Example: Freelance consultant, local shop

2. Partnership

Owners: Two or more individuals
Liability: Partners share unlimited liability
Tax: Pass-through taxation
Example: Law firms, medical practices

3. Corporation

Owners: Shareholders
Liability: Limited to investment amount
Tax: Separate entity taxation
Example: Apple, Microsoft, large companies

üè¢ More Business Structures

4. Limited Liability Company (LLC)

Owners: Members
Liability: Limited liability protection
Tax: Flexible (can choose pass-through or corporate)
Benefit: Combines partnership flexibility with corporation protection

5. Cooperative (Co-op)

Owners: Members who use services
Control: Democratic (one member, one vote)
Example: Credit unions, agricultural co-ops

Ease of Formation: Sole Prop > Partnership > LLC > Corporation

Liability Protection: Corporation = LLC > Partnership = Sole Prop

Tax Complexity: Corporation > LLC > Partnership > Sole Prop

üí∞ Financial Transactions

Events that have a monetary impact on financial statements and must be recorded

Types of Transactions

Cash Transactions: Immediate exchange of cash
Credit Transactions: Payment deferred to future date
Internal Transactions: Within business (depreciation, adjustments)
External Transactions: With outside parties (sales, purchases)

Transaction Cycle

1. Transaction occurs
2. Source document created
3. Journal entry recorded
4. Posted to ledger
5. Trial balance prepared
6. Financial statements created
