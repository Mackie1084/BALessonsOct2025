📖 The General Journal

What is a General Journal?

The General Journal is the book of original entry where all financial transactions are first recorded in chronological order before being posted to the ledger accounts.

Purpose of the General Journal

✓ Provides a complete chronological record of all transactions
✓ Shows the debit and credit for each transaction
✓ Includes explanations (narrations) for each entry
✓ Creates an audit trail for verification
✓ Helps prevent and detect errors

Components of a Journal Entry

Date: When the transaction occurred
Account Titles: Accounts affected by the transaction
Debit Amount: Amount debited to account(s)
Credit Amount: Amount credited to account(s)
Narration: Brief explanation of the transaction

🇸🇬 Goods and Services Tax (GST)

What is GST in Singapore?

GST is a consumption tax levied on the supply of goods and services in Singapore, as well as imports.

Current Rate: 9% (as of 2024)

Administered by: Inland Revenue Authority of Singapore (IRAS)

GST Registration

Mandatory: When taxable turnover exceeds SGD $1 million in the past 12 months, or is expected to exceed SGD $1 million in the next 12 months

Voluntary: Businesses can register even below the threshold

Input Tax

GST paid on purchases and expenses (recoverable)

Output Tax

GST collected on sales (payable to IRAS)

Net GST Payable = Output Tax - Input Tax

💰 GST Calculation Examples

Example 1: Sales Transaction

Scenario: Your Singapore company sells goods worth SGD $10,000

• Selling Price: SGD $10,000
• GST @ 9%: SGD $900
• Total Invoice Amount: SGD $10,900

• Purchase Price: SGD $2,000

Your company can claim SGD $180 as Input Tax (recoverable from IRAS)

⚖️ Understanding Debits and Credits

The Golden Rule

For every transaction: DEBITS = CREDITS

DEBIT (Dr)

Increases:

• Assets
• Expenses
• Drawings

Decreases:

• Liabilities
• Equity
• Revenue

CREDIT (Cr)

Increases:

• Liabilities
• Equity
• Revenue

Decreases:

• Assets
• Expenses
• Drawings

Memory Aid: DEALOR

Debits: Dividends, Expenses, Assets, Losses
Credits: Liabilities, Owner's Equity, Revenues

📚 General Ledger

What is a General Ledger?

The General Ledger is a collection of all accounts used by a business. Journal entries are posted (transferred) to individual ledger accounts.

Purpose of the General Ledger

✓ Organizes transactions by account
✓ Shows the running balance of each account
✓ Makes it easy to see all transactions affecting a specific account
✓ Provides data for financial statements

Posting Process

1. Identify accounts affected in journal entry
2. Transfer debit amounts to debit side of respective accounts
3. Transfer credit amounts to credit side of respective accounts
4. Calculate running balance after each transaction
5. Reference journal page number for audit trail

⚖️ Trial Balance

What is a Trial Balance?

A Trial Balance is a statement that lists all ledger accounts with their debit or credit balances at a specific date. It verifies that total debits equal total credits.

Purpose of Trial Balance

✓ Verifies the arithmetic accuracy of ledger postings
✓ Ensures debits equal credits
✓ Provides a summary of all account balances
✓ Serves as the basis for preparing financial statements
✓ Helps identify posting errors

Important Notes

⚠️ A balanced trial balance does NOT guarantee error-free books
⚠️ Some errors may still exist (e.g., wrong account used, omitted entries)
⚠️ It only confirms mathematical accuracy

⚠️ Common Accounting Errors

Errors Revealed by Trial Balance

1. Single Entry: Recording only debit or credit
2. Unequal Amounts: Debit and credit amounts don't match
3. Posting Error: Wrong amount posted to ledger
4. Calculation Error: Wrong balance computed
5. Omission in Posting: One side not posted to ledger

Errors NOT Revealed by Trial Balance

1. Error of Omission: Transaction completely left out
2. Error of Commission: Posted to wrong account of same type
3. Error of Principle: Wrong type of account used
4. Compensating Errors: Two errors cancel each other out
5. Error of Original Entry: Wrong amount in journal

Singapore Context Example:

Error of Principle: Recording SGD $5,000 furniture purchase as Rent Expense instead of Asset
→ Trial balance will still balance, but financial statements will be incorrect!

🎯 Summary - The Accounting Cycle

1. Transaction Occurs

Business event takes place (sale, purchase, payment, etc.)

2. Record in General Journal

Create journal entry with date, accounts, amounts, and narration
Remember GST: Separate Input Tax and Output Tax

3. Post to General Ledger

Transfer amounts to individual T-accounts
Calculate running balances

4. Prepare Trial Balance

List all account balances
Verify Debits = Credits
Calculate Net GST payable/refundable

5. Prepare Financial Statements

Income Statement, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow Statement

🇸🇬 GST Filing in Singapore

GST F5 Return Filing

Frequency: Quarterly (every 3 months)
Due Date: 1 month after end of accounting period
Method: Electronic filing via myTax Portal (IRAS)

Information Required in GST F5

1. Total value of standard-rated supplies (Output Tax)
2. Total value of zero-rated supplies
3. Total value of exempt supplies
4. Total amount of input tax claimable
5. Net GST payable or refundable

Example: Quarterly GST Calculation

Total Sales (excl GST) SGD 150,000
Output Tax @ 9% SGD 13,500
Total Purchases (excl GST) SGD 90,000
Input Tax @ 9% SGD 8,100
NET GST PAYABLE SGD 5,400

Payment

GST must be paid to IRAS via GIRO, internet banking, or at AXS stations before the due date to avoid penalties

💡 Practical Tips for Singapore Businesses

1. Maintain Proper Documentation

• Keep all tax invoices for GST claims
• Retain records for at least 5 years (IRAS requirement)
• Ensure invoices show GST registration number
• Proper invoice format with all required details

2. GST Invoice Requirements

Must include:
• Words "Tax Invoice" or "GST Invoice"
• Seller's GST registration number
• Invoice number and date
• Customer's name and address
• Description of goods/services
• GST amount charged

3. Accounting Software

Popular in Singapore:
• Xero • QuickBooks • MYOB
• Autocount • SQL Account
All support GST calculations and IRAS filing

4. Professional Support

Consider engaging:
• Certified Public Accountant (CPA)
• Tax agent for GST filing
• Bookkeeper for daily entries
